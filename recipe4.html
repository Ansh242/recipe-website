Punjabi Chana Masala

Prep Time: 10 Mins Cook Time: 30 Mins Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, blended into a puree
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1 tbsp Chole Masala powder (or Garam Masala)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Temper the Spices: Heat oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and bay leaf. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn a deep golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and the slit green chili, cooking for another minute.
  3. Cook the Tomatoes: Pour in the tomato puree. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook this mixture, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the oil starts to separate.
  4. Add the Masala: Stir in the Chole Masala powder (or Garam Masala) and cook for 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  5. Simmer the Chickpeas: Add the drained chickpeas to the pan. Pour in 1 cup of water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes so the chickpeas absorb the flavors.
  6. Mash and Garnish: Lightly mash a few of the chickpeas with the back of your spoon against the side of the pot to thicken the gravy. Garnish generously with fresh cilantro and serve with bhatura or rice.
