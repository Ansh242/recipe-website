Classic Gulab Jamun

Prep Time: 20 Mins Cook Time: 25 Mins Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • 3-4 tbsp warm milk
  • 1.5 cups sugar (for syrup)
  • 1.5 cups water (for syrup)
  • 4 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 tsp rose water
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the Sugar Syrup: In a wide pan, combine sugar, water, and crushed cardamom. Boil for 5-7 minutes until the syrup becomes slightly sticky (no thread consistency needed). Turn off the heat and stir in the rose water. Set aside to keep warm.
  2. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add the ghee and mix well until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Bind the Dough: Gradually add warm milk, 1 tablespoon at a time, and gently gather the mixture into a soft, sticky dough. Do not over-knead. Let it rest for 5 minutes.
  4. Shape the Balls: Grease your palms with a little ghee. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls with no cracks.
  5. Fry the Jamuns: Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over low heat. Gently drop the dough balls into the oil. Fry slowly on low heat, turning continuously, until they are an even, dark golden brown.
  6. Soak in Syrup: Remove the fried balls with a slotted spoon, drain excess oil, and immediately drop them into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 2 hours before serving.
