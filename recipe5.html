Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani

Prep Time: 45 Mins Cook Time: 45 Mins Difficulty: Hard

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain Basmati rice, soaked for 30 mins
  • 1.5 lbs bone-in chicken pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and deep-fried (Birista)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices: 4 cloves, 2 cardamom pods, 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tbsp Biryani Masala, 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, 1/2 cup cilantro
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 3 tbsp ghee

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, half of the fried onions, mint, cilantro, Biryani masala, chili powder, and salt. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Parboil the Rice: Boil a large pot of water with heavy salt and the whole spices (cloves, cardamom, cinnamon). Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until it is 70% done (about 5-6 minutes). Drain the water immediately.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Heat a heavy-bottomed pot (or Dutch oven) with 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add the marinated chicken and cook on high heat for 10 minutes to sear the meat and reduce the liquid.
  4. Layering (Dum): Turn the heat to the lowest setting. Spread the partially cooked chicken into an even layer at the bottom of the pot. Layer the parboiled rice evenly over the chicken.
  5. Garnish the Layers: Pour the saffron milk evenly over the top of the rice. Sprinkle the remaining fried onions, mint, and cilantro. Drizzle the last tablespoon of melted ghee over the top.
  6. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (seal the edges with dough or aluminum foil if needed). Cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening. Gently fluff the rice and chicken together before serving with raita.
